Q1: 1. Describe your Disaster Recovery (DR) and Business Continuity Plan (BCP).
A: AWS has a comprehensive Business Continuity Plan (BCP) and Disaster Recovery (DR) strategy, underpinned by formal policies and procedures that are established, documented, approved, communicated, applied, evaluated, and maintained, with annual reviews by leadership. These plans are designed to ensure minimum outage time and maximum effectiveness of recovery and reconstitution efforts, aligning with ISO 27001 standards.

Our approach to operational resilience encompasses processes and procedures to identify, respond to, and recover from major events. The BCP details a three-phased approach: Activation and Notification, Recovery, and Reconstitution. AWS infrastructure is built to physical, environmental, and security standards in an active-active configuration, employing an n+1 redundancy model across data centers and components to eliminate single points of failure and ensure system availability. Data centers are online and serving traffic, with sufficient capacity to load-balance traffic to remaining sites in case of failure.

AWS maintains retention policies for internal data and critical system components, which are replicated across multiple Availability Zones, and backups are maintained and monitored for confidentiality, integrity, and availability. Our disaster response plan is established for natural and man-made disasters, updated at least annually, and exercised annually through GameDay Exercises.

Under the shared responsibility model, AWS is responsible for the resilience of the cloud infrastructure, while customers are responsible for implementing their own robust continuity plans, including frequent server instance backups, data redundancy replication, and placing instances and storing data across multiple geographic regions and Availability Zones. Customers are also responsible for their backup and retention policies, leveraging AWS services like AWS Backup for centralized management and automation. All relevant documentation is developed, identified, acquired, available to authorized stakeholders, and reviewed periodically, with established communication channels for stakeholders.

For other customer-generated logs and content, data retention policies are the responsibility of the customer, in accordance with the AWS Shared Responsibility Model.
